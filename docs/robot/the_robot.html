The Robot¶

The hexapod robot is the central artefact of this project. It consists of a central body frame, six identical leg assemblies, an electronics bay, and an antenna mast — all fabricated from 3-D printed PLA components bolted together with M3 hardware.

Each leg has three degrees of freedom, actuated by hobby-grade servo motors:

  • Coxa joint — rotates the leg horizontally about a vertical axis, controlling the sweep angle.

  • Femur joint — lifts the leg up and down relative to the body plane.

  • Tibia joint — extends or retracts the lower leg segment, controlling foot reach.

The body houses the main microcontroller, power-distribution board, and receiver module in a compact electronics bay accessible via a removable lid panel. The centre of mass was deliberately kept low by positioning the heaviest components (battery pack, power board) at the base of the body cavity.
