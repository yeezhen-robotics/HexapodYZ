======
Mounts
======

   - Replace ``_static/cad_mounts.png`` with your servo-mount or bracket CAD screenshot.

   :width: 600px
   :align: center

The hexapod uses a family of mount components to interface servos, the camera module, and the
antenna mast to the structural frame. All mounts share a common two-bolt M2 attachment footprint
so they are interchangeable on the body's mounting rail.

Servo Mounts
-------------

Each servo is retained by a dedicated clamp-style mount that surrounds three sides of the servo
body. The fourth side is open to allow the servo cable to exit without strain. Two M2 bolts
clamp the two halves of the mount together; a locating ledge on the inner face seats against
the servo's flange to resist axial pull-out.

Mounts are provided in three orientations:

- **Horizontal mount** — for the coxa servos (rotation axis vertical).
- **Vertical mount (forward-facing)** — for the femur servos.
- **Vertical mount (rearward-facing)** — for the tibia servos.

The three orientations are derived from a single master sketch by applying directional
suppression patterns, keeping the part family compact.

Camera Mount
-------------

The front camera module is retained by a ball-and-socket tilt mechanism that allows ±20°
vertical adjustment. The ball is printed in PETG for its self-lubricating properties; the
socket is PLA. A single M3 friction bolt locks the desired tilt angle in place.

Antenna Mast Mount
-------------------

The antenna mast is a press-fit cylinder that inserts into a pocket on the rear-left corner
of the body. A small M2 grub screw on the side of the pocket prevents the mast from rotating
or vibrating loose during operation. The mast height was selected to keep the antenna at least
50 mm above the body's highest point to reduce near-field interference with the electronics.
