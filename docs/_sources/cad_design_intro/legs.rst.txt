==========================
Legs — Coxa, Femur, Tibia
==========================

Each of the six legs is composed of three links — the **coxa**, **femur**, and **tibia** —
connected by revolute joints actuated by hobby-grade servo motors. All six legs share identical
part files; mirroring is handled in the assembly rather than by creating separate left- and
right-hand variants.

Coxa
-----

The coxa is the hip segment. It attaches directly to the body's coxa servo mount and spans
horizontally from the body side to the femur servo. The coxa rotation axis is vertical,
giving the leg its lateral sweep angle.

- **Length:** *TBD mm* (centre-to-centre)
- **Cross-section:** Hollow rectangular, *TBD × TBD mm* outer, *TBD mm* wall
- **Servo interface:** M2 bolt-through clamp on the proximal end; servo horn press-fit on the distal end

Femur
------

.. Note on the image:
   - Replace ``_static/cad_femur.png`` with your femur CAD screenshot.

.. figure:: /_static/cad_femur.png
   :width: 500px
   :align: center
   :alt: Femur link CAD model

   *Femur link — replace with your own render.*

The femur connects the coxa to the tibia. Its rotation axis is horizontal and perpendicular
to the body's longitudinal axis, giving the leg its lift (elevation) angle. The femur is the
longest link and therefore the primary contributor to foot reachable-workspace depth.

- **Length:** *TBD mm* (centre-to-centre)
- **Cross-section:** I-beam profile to maximise second moment of area in the bending plane
- **Servo interface:** Servo horn press-fit with M2 retention screw on the proximal end

Tibia
------

.. Note on the image:
   - Replace ``_static/cad_tibia.png`` with your tibia CAD screenshot.

.. figure:: /_static/cad_tibia.png

   :align: center

The tibia is the lower leg. It connects the femur to the foot tip. The tibia rotation axis
is parallel to the femur axis, allowing the leg to extend or retract the foot along an arc
in the sagittal plane. The distal end terminates in a hemispherical foot tip that minimises
contact-point variation with terrain irregularities.

- **Length:** *TBD mm* (centre-to-centre)
- **Cross-section:** Solid circular, *TBD mm* diameter, with a chamfered base for overhang-free printing
- **Foot tip:** Hemispherical, *TBD mm* radius; printed separately in TPU for compliant ground contact
