======================
3 Versions of Design
======================

The mechanical design of the hexapod went through three major iterations before reaching
the final configuration. Each version addressed specific shortcomings identified either
in simulation or through physical prototyping.

Version 1 — Proof of Concept
------------------------------

.. Note on the image below:
   - Replace ``_static/cad_v1.png`` with your Version 1 CAD screenshot or render.
   - Adjust ``:width:`` as needed (e.g. ``600px``).

.. figure:: /_static/cad_v1.png
   :width: 600px
   :align: center
   :alt: CAD Design Version 1

   *Version 1 CAD model — replace with your own screenshot or render.*

Version 1 established the basic geometry: a rectangular body with six legs arranged in two
rows of three. Servo mounts were designed for press-fit attachment, which proved insufficiently
rigid under the dynamic loads of the tripod gait. The leg links were initially under-dimensioned,
resulting in noticeable deflection at the tibia tip under the robot's own weight.

**Key issues identified:**

- Press-fit servo mounts loosened after repeated actuation cycles.
- Tibia link wall thickness insufficient — visible flex under load.
- Electronics bay inaccessible without full disassembly of the body.

Version 2 — Structural Refinement
-----------------------------------

.. Note on the image below:
   - Replace ``_static/cad_v2.png`` with your Version 2 CAD screenshot or render.

.. figure:: /_static/cad_v2.png
   :width: 600px
   :align: center
   :alt: CAD Design Version 2

   *Version 2 CAD model — replace with your own screenshot or render.*

Version 2 addressed the structural deficiencies identified in Version 1. Servo mounts were
redesigned with M2 bolt-through interfaces. The tibia wall thickness was increased by 1.5 mm
and the cross-section transitioned to a hollow-rectangular profile for better specific
stiffness. An access panel was added to the top of the body, enabling the electronics bay
to be opened without disturbing the leg assemblies.

**Key issues remaining:**

- Centre of mass too far forward, causing pitch instability at speed.
- Coxa joint range limited by body-frame geometry — only ±40° achievable.
- Antenna mast obstructed the camera field of view.

Version 3 — Final Iteration
-----------------------------

.. Note on the image below:
   - Replace ``_static/cad_v3.png`` with your Version 3 CAD screenshot or render.

.. figure:: /_static/cad_v3.png
   :width: 600px
   :align: center
   :alt: CAD Design Version 3

   *Version 3 CAD model — replace with your own screenshot or render.*

Version 3 is the design that was fabricated and documented in this project. The battery was
repositioned to the rear of the body cavity, correcting the centre-of-mass offset. Body
clearances around the coxa joints were enlarged to allow ±70° sweep. The antenna mast was
relocated to the rear-left of the body, clearing the camera's forward field of view.

A detailed explanation of the final design follows in the next section.
