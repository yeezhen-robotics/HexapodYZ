==========================
Coming to the Final Model
==========================

The progression from Version 1 to the final model was guided by a consistent evaluation
framework: after each design iteration, the model was imported into CoppeliaSim and the tripod
gait was re-run to check for interference and stability regressions. Parts were also briefly
stress-tested in a simple FEA (finite element analysis) tool to flag any regions with
stress concentrations above the PLA yield strength.

Decision Criteria
------------------

Each design revision was assessed against four criteria before being accepted:

1. **Print feasibility** — can every part be printed without support material on the faces that
   mate with other components? Minimising support reduces post-processing time and improves
   surface quality at mating interfaces.
2. **Structural adequacy** — do peak stresses under the expected servo torques remain below
   50 % of the PLA yield strength (a 2× safety factor)?
3. **Assembly access** — can all fasteners be reached with a standard hex driver without
   removing other components?
4. **Gait clearance** — does the leg geometry provide adequate ground clearance in all
   simulated gait configurations, including the edge-case step heights?

Only when all four criteria were satisfied was an iteration accepted as a candidate for
fabrication.
