======================
Explanation on the Design
======================

This section provides a concise rationale for the principal design choices in the final model.
Detailed part-level descriptions are given in the :ref:`cad_design_intro` chapter.

Body Frame Geometry
--------------------

The body was designed as a closed hexagonal shell rather than an open rectangular tray. The
hexagonal perimeter more closely matches the natural attachment geometry of six equidistant
legs and provides a shorter torque path from each leg mount to the central spine, reducing
torsional flex during asymmetric loading (e.g. when only one tripod is in contact with the
ground).

Leg Proportions
----------------

The coxa : femur : tibia length ratio was set to **1 : 2 : 2.5** based on a survey of
biological hexapods and prior robotic platforms. This ratio provides a large reachable
workspace relative to the body footprint while keeping the femur and tibia servo torque
requirements within the rated output of the chosen motors.

Servo Mount Interface
----------------------

All servo mounts use a two-bolt M2 clamping interface with a locating ledge. The ledge
absorbs the majority of the shear load from servo reaction torque, leaving the bolts to
resist only pull-out forces. This substantially extends the fatigue life of the printed
mount compared to a bolt-only interface.

Electronics Bay
----------------

The electronics bay occupies the central 60 % of the body volume. It is accessible via a
snap-fit lid on the dorsal surface. The MCU, receiver module, and power-distribution board
are mounted on a sub-tray that can be removed as a single unit — useful for bench testing
or swapping between robot bodies.

Cable Management
-----------------

Servo cables are routed through dedicated channels moulded into the coxa and femur links,
keeping them away from the joint rotation axes. At the body entry point, a grommet-lined
aperture prevents chafing. This routing strategy was validated in simulation by animating
the full range of joint motion and checking for cable-interference conflicts.
