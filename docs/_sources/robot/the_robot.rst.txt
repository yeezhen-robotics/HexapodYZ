==========
The Robot
==========

.. raw:: html

   <div style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; gap: 2rem; margin-bottom: 2rem;">

     <!-- LEFT: text -->
     <div style="flex: 1;">

The hexapod robot is the central artefact of this project. It consists of a central body
frame, six identical leg assemblies, an electronics bay, and an antenna mast — all fabricated
from 3-D printed PLA components bolted together with M3 hardware.

Each leg has three degrees of freedom, actuated by hobby-grade servo motors:

- **Coxa joint** — rotates the leg horizontally about a vertical axis, controlling the
  sweep angle.
- **Femur joint** — lifts the leg up and down relative to the body plane.
- **Tibia joint** — extends or retracts the lower leg segment, controlling foot reach.

The body houses the main microcontroller, power-distribution board, and receiver module in
a compact electronics bay accessible via a removable lid panel. The centre of mass was
deliberately kept low by positioning the heaviest components (battery pack, power board)
at the base of the body cavity.

across opposing legs, and stands **XX mm** tall in its nominal stance.
