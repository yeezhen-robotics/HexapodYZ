Project Development Overview
-----------------------------

The hexapod was developed in four broad phases, each building on the last:

**Phase 1 — Concept & Simulation.**
Before any hardware was purchased, the locomotion strategy was prototyped in CoppeliaSim.
A simplified three-link leg model was constructed in the simulator and the tripod gait was
validated against static-stability criteria. Serial communication between CoppeliaSim and an
Arduino platform allowed real controller code to drive the virtual robot, exposing timing and
protocol issues early and cheaply.

**Phase 2 — Mechanical Design.**
With a validated gait in hand, the structural design was tackled across three CAD iterations.
Master sketches in the parametric CAD environment ensured that changes to core dimensions
(leg length, body width, servo-mount geometry) propagated automatically through the entire
assembly. The final design was exported as STL files and 3-D printed in PLA.

**Phase 3 — Electronics Integration.**
The servo bus, power distribution board, microcontroller, and antenna module were assembled
and bench-tested. A custom power-distribution PCB was designed to supply regulated 5 V to the
servo rail while keeping the logic supply isolated, preventing servo noise from corrupting the
MCU.

**Phase 4 — Software & Tuning.**
The inverse kinematics solver, tripod gait scheduler, and signal-compaction layer were
implemented on the target MCU. The robot was progressively tuned on flat ground, then
tested on inclines and rough surfaces until consistent, stable locomotion was achieved.
