.. _servos:

======
Servos
======

The hexapod uses eighteen servo motors — three per leg — to actuate its joints. Servo
selection was one of the most consequential hardware decisions of the project, as the
chosen motors directly determine the robot's payload capacity, speed, and battery runtime.

Selection Criteria
-------------------

Candidate servos were evaluated against four criteria:

1. **Stall torque** — must exceed the calculated peak joint torque (including a 2× safety factor)
   under the robot's maximum expected load.
2. **Speed** — must complete a full-range sweep within the gait step period at the target
   walking speed.
3. **Current draw** — aggregate stall current of all eighteen servos must not exceed the
   power-distribution board's rated output.
4. **Form factor** — must fit within the designed servo-mount pocket dimensions without
   modification.

PWM Control
------------

All servos are controlled via standard 50 Hz PWM signals. Pulse widths of 500 µs and 2500 µs
correspond to the servo's minimum and maximum angular positions respectively, with 1500 µs
representing the neutral (centre) position. The MCU generates PWM outputs using dedicated
hardware timers to ensure jitter-free pulse edges at all times.

To avoid the current surge that occurs when all eighteen servos are commanded simultaneously,
the firmware staggers servo updates across consecutive control ticks, limiting the instantaneous
current demand on the power rail.
