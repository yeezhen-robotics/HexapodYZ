=========================
Power Distribution Board
=========================

The power distribution board (PDB) regulates and distributes power from the main battery to
the servo rail, the MCU, and the wireless module. Separating the servo supply from the logic
supply is essential: under load, servo motors generate voltage spikes and current transients
that can corrupt MCU operation if the two rails share the same supply path without adequate
decoupling.

Board Description
------------------

The PDB accepts the raw LiPo cell voltage on its input terminals and provides two regulated
output rails:

- **Servo rail (5 V / ~10 A)** — supplies all eighteen servos via a shared power bus.
  A high-current switching regulator (buck converter) steps the LiPo voltage down to a stable
  5 V regardless of cell state-of-charge.
- **Logic rail (3.3 V / 500 mA)** — supplies the MCU and nRF24L01+ module via a low-dropout
  linear regulator. The LDO is powered from the servo-rail output rather than raw battery
  voltage to reduce its headroom requirement and therefore heat dissipation.

Key protection features include:

- **Reverse-polarity protection** — a P-channel MOSFET in the battery input path prevents
  damage if the battery connector is accidentally reversed.
- **Bulk capacitance** — 4 × 1000 µF electrolytic capacitors distributed across the servo
  rail suppress transient voltage dips during servo stall events.
- **Filtered logic supply** — a 100 µH inductor and 100 µF capacitor form an LC filter on
  the LDO input, attenuating high-frequency switching noise from the buck converter before
  it reaches the MCU.
