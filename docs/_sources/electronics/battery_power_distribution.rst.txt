=================================
Battery Power Distribution Board
=================================

The battery power distribution board (BPDB) is the interface between the LiPo battery pack
and the rest of the power system. It provides the main power switch, battery voltage monitoring,
and the low-battery cutoff circuit that protects the LiPo cells from over-discharge — a
condition that permanently damages LiPo chemistry if the cell voltage falls below ~3.0 V per cell.

Board Description
------------------

The BPDB performs three functions:

**1. Main power switching.**
A latching push-button switch (or slide switch, depending on revision) connects and
disconnects the battery from the downstream PDB. A high-side P-channel MOSFET rated for the
full system current is placed in series with the switch to protect the switch contacts from
arcing under load.

**2. Battery voltage monitoring.**
A resistor-divider network scales the battery terminal voltage to the MCU's ADC input range.
The MCU samples this voltage at 1 Hz and broadcasts the battery state-of-charge over the
wireless link to the controller display. A green LED on the BPDB illuminates when the battery
is above 80 % and transitions to amber below 40 %, providing a local visual indicator
independent of the wireless link.

**3. Low-battery cutoff.**
A dedicated voltage-comparator circuit monitors the battery terminal voltage. When the
voltage falls below the configured cutoff threshold (corresponding to ~3.2 V per LiPo cell),
the comparator output drives the MOSFET gate low, disconnecting the load and protecting the
cells. The cutoff is latching — the system must be manually power-cycled after the battery is
recharged to restore operation.
